Resources

These can help you gain a better sense of self-image and confidence

8 Keys to Recovery from an Eating Disorder by Carolyn Costin and Gwen Grabb is a detailed guide for people considering recovery from an ED. This book covers various topics, from overcoming guilt to entering therapy.

Body Image: Understanding Body Dissatisfaction in Men, Women, and Children by Sarah Grogan takes a scientific perspective on body image issues.

Love Yourself is a beautiful video that points out the unnatural beauty standards in the media

The Messy Heads is, in addition to it being a published magazine, a blog with daily updates run by social media personality, Emma Mercury. The premise of this magazine/blog is to promote the idea that you shouldn’t seek perfection in life because it is unattainable.

Kati Morton's Eating Disorder workbooks are a great resource for recovery. Check our her website for more workbooks, worksheets, and a supportve community.

The National Institutes for Mental Health provides information about eating disorders, such as what an eating disorder is and risk factors.

About Face is a website that educates individuals about body image and its portrayal in the media. It provides resources for taking action against advertisement-makers that set unrealistic body standards in their advertisements.

National Eating Disorders Association is a group that provides resources, such as programs and services for families, to those struggling with eating disorders. It also educates the public about eating disorders.

The Messy Heads is, in addition to it being a published magazine, a blog with daily updates that is run by Emma Mercury. The premise of this magazine/blog is to promote the idea that you shouldn’t see perfection in life because it is unattainable. It also includes art and literature submissions from people across the world.

Do Something is an organization that helps to provide solutions to a number of issues, such as bullying, violence, homelessness and body image.
