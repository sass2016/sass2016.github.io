Facts and Statistics about Body Image

"Body image" is the way someone identifies their body and the way one believes that others identify them. Body image is influenced by a number of factors: social media, family, freinds, environment, self-esteem, and more.

Negative perception of one's body or appearance can ultimately lead to low self-esteem, eating disorders, as well as mental health issues.

According to DoSomething, 95% of people with eating disorders are between the ages of 12 and 25. Approxiametely 10% of people with eating disorders in general seek no professional help.

Furthermore, only 5% of women naturally possess the body type often portrayed by Americans in media and popular culture. Over 90% of teenage girls want to change at least one asppect of their physical appearance and many would consider undergong cosmetic surgery when they're older.

Because ideal physical standards for boys and girls are often unacheivable, people resort to strenuos diet and exercise, cosmetic surgery, and even thoughts of suicide to cope with the negative view of their appearance.

34% of adolescent boys and 49% of girls admit to having been on a diet to lose weight or alter their body.

Males and females are pressured by American media and popular culture with the notion that outward perfection equals success and self-confidence. These outlets establish vivid standards of physical beauty that can be hard to defy.
